## Summary

Describes the original Mutalyzer tool for standardising genetic variant descriptions using HGVS guidelines. Applied to over 11,000 variants across three mutation databases (PAH, BIC BRCA2, HbVar), finding error rates of 13%, 75%, and 62% respectively. Attributes high error rates to insufficient genomic reference sequences or non-compliance with HGVS standards. Emphasises that unambiguous variant descriptions are essential to prevent clinical misinterpretation.

## Relevance to cdot

Establishes the longstanding problem of variant description inconsistency that the entire HGVS tooling ecosystem (including cdot) is designed to address. Historically important as the first widely used HGVS checker. The requirement for "well-annotated genomic reference sequences" to achieve accurate checking is exactly what cdot provides.
