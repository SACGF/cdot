## Summary

Retrospective and current-state overview of RefSeq's 25-year history. Describes how RefSeq leverages automatic processes and expert curation to create robust reference sequences. Covers advances from advanced sequencing technologies, RNA-Seq integration for annotation improvement, and expansion of taxonomic and functional coverage. Reflects on the database's role as the canonical source for genome, transcript, and protein sequence standards.

## Relevance to cdot

RefSeq is cdot's most important data source for human clinical genomics (NM_/NR_ accessions are what appear in HGVS strings from clinical labs). This paper's perspective on 25 years of curation provides context for why historical RefSeq annotation releases matter and why cdot's approach of incorporating many historical GFF3 releases (rather than just the latest) is important for resolving old clinical HGVS strings.
