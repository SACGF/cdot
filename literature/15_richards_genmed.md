## Summary

The foundational ACMG/AMP guidelines for sequence variant interpretation. Establishes five-tier variant classification (pathogenic, likely pathogenic, uncertain significance, likely benign, benign) with defined evidence criteria using population data, computational predictions, functional studies, and segregation data. Became the de-facto standard for clinical variant classification in laboratories worldwide and the basis for ClinVar's classification system.

## Relevance to cdot

These guidelines assume that variants can be expressed using HGVS nomenclature and that transcript coordinates are reliable and reproducible. Clinical labs applying these guidelines need accurate HGVS conversion — the very function cdot enables. The widespread adoption of these guidelines has dramatically increased demand for HGVS tooling. The paper is the upstream clinical context that makes the cdot infrastructure problem worth solving.
